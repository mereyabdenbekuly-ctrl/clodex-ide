# Clodex Code of Conduct

Clodex is a technical community where people should be able to contribute without
harassment, intimidation, or pressure. Participation requires professional,
honest, and respectful conduct.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss the work, evidence, and trade-offs; do not attack the person.
- Give specific, actionable feedback and receive disagreement without retaliation.
- Respect names, pronouns, boundaries, privacy, time, and different levels of
  experience.
- State uncertainty, authorship, affiliations, funding, and conflicts of interest
  when they are relevant to a decision.
- Preserve credit for upstream and community work.
- Stop or move a conversation when a moderator asks, and use private reporting
  channels for vulnerabilities or sensitive personal information.
- Help correct harm when possible, even when it was unintended.

## Unacceptable conduct

The following are not permitted:

- discrimination, degrading remarks, or exclusion based on identity, background,
  disability, appearance, nationality, belief, or personal circumstances;
- insults, personal attacks, threats, deliberate intimidation, stalking, or
  repeated contact after a request to stop;
- unwanted sexual attention or sexualized content directed at another person;
- publishing private information or communications without permission, except
  when confidentially reporting abuse or a credible safety risk;
- impersonation, false claims of authority or authorship, deceptive endorsements,
  review manipulation, or coordinated abuse of community metrics;
- sabotaging work, concealing a known security risk, retaliating against a
  reporter, or using project access outside its approved purpose;
- sustained disruption, spam, or knowingly consuming project resources for an
  unrelated purpose.

Reasonable technical criticism, moderation, security controls, and rejection of
a contribution are not harassment when applied consistently and communicated
professionally.

## Scope

This policy applies in repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, project
chat, events, and private project communications. It also applies when someone is
using an official project role, account, or event affiliation in another space.

## Reporting

Do not open a public issue about conduct involving private or identifying details.
Use GitHub's reporting tools or email **support@clodex.xyz** with the subject
`[CONDUCT]` and request a private reporting channel. If the report concerns the
founding maintainer while no uninvolved Core Maintainer exists, use GitHub's
platform reporting tools and do not send the report to the subject. Suspected
software vulnerabilities must instead follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

A useful report includes what happened, when and where it happened, relevant
links or screenshots, witnesses if any, and any immediate safety concern. Share
only information needed to assess the report. Knowingly false reports are a
violation; an unproven good-faith report is not.

Reports will be handled by people without a material conflict of interest. They
will limit disclosure, preserve relevant evidence, give the affected parties a
fair opportunity to respond when safe, and communicate an outcome to the reporter
and subject as appropriate. Retaliation for a good-faith report or participation
in a review is prohibited.

## Enforcement

Depending on severity, pattern, and risk, project leaders may apply:

1. private guidance or a request to correct behavior;
2. a formal warning with stated expectations;
3. temporary limits on communication, assignments, or repository access;
4. removal of roles or access, or a permanent ban from project spaces.

Steps do not have to be applied in order. Credible threats, doxxing, account
compromise, serious harassment, or security abuse may require immediate protective
action. Access suspension during a review is not by itself a final finding.

An affected person may request an appeal within 14 days by contacting an
uninvolved Core Maintainer. During the solo-maintainer bootstrap, the project
will request an external, non-conflicted reviewer when practical; if none is
available, the limitation and final decision are documented. The appeal must
identify a factual or procedural error. The reviewer may uphold, change, or
reverse the outcome and will record the decision privately, with a public
summary only when it is safe and useful.

Project leaders are subject to the same standards and may not review reports or
appeals in which they are involved.
